Using CVS on MS Windows in NCBI

  1. You may have a pre-installed CVS executable on your PC, for example in C:\WINNT\System32\cvs.exe. If not, then download it from \\Basie\IEB\cvs.exe (you also can find it at http://www.cyclic.com/) and copy to C:\WINNT\System32\cvs.exe.
  2. Create environment variable CVSROOT:
    • Click the right mouse button on the icon of your NT PC name (it is usually located in the upper left corner of NT desktop), and select item "Properties".
    • Form "System Properties shows up. -- Here, choose folder "Environment" and click on the lower window ("User Variables for Yourname:") on the string with TEMP variable.
    • Now, type "CVSROOT" in the "Variable:" text field, then type something like :server:yourlogname@yourworkstation:/am/src/NCBI/vault.ncbi in the "Value:" text field. Here, the yourlogname stands for your NCBI account name with all letter lowercased. :server:beloslyu.dove.nlm.nih.gov@ray:/am/src/NCBI/vault.ncbi
    • Press the "Set" button. The new variable CVSROOT and its value should appear in the lower window("User Variables for Yourname:").
    • Press the "Apply" button.
    • Press the "OK" button.
    • Logout, then login to your PC again.
  3. Login to machine "yourworkstation" and add the following two lines to your ~/.rhosts file:
    • pcname yourlogname (for example: dnb beloslyu)
    • pcname Yourlogname (for example: dnb Beloslyu)
    where the pcname stands for your PC name, yourlogname is your NCBI UNIX domain account name(usually all lowercased), and Yourlogname is your NCBI NT domain account name(usually it is just like the UNIX domain account name, but with the first letter uppercased).
    NOTE: Make sure that you have ncbi_tools facility activated in your ~/.ncbi_hints file!
